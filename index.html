About Us

Muslim students across New Jersey often have to fight, school by school, for accommodations that should already exist: a place to pray on Fridays, and recognition of Eid as a day their faith requires them to be absent. Prayer For All exists to make that fight easier and less isolating, by giving students the templates, guidance, and precedent they need to make the case at their own school. Success isn't a single win, it's these accommodations becoming standard practice at schools across the state, carried forward by students themselves rather than depending on any one person or team.

How We Started. Prayer For All began through the NJMVP and Ali Leadership Institute Youth Leadership Program, where our team set out to solve a problem we'd seen firsthand: Muslim students without a Friday prayer space or recognized Eid absence at their own school. After proving the model worked at one New Jersey public school, we realized the process could be documented and handed off, so any student, at any school, could run the same playbook without needing us to do it for them. What started as one school's win is now something we're actively building out across the state.

Questions You Might Have

Is asking for this even allowed?

Yes. Students at public schools have the right to pray on their own time, and requesting a space to do it is a normal accommodation ask, one that schools in NJ have already granted. For Eid specifically, New Jersey law (N.J.S.A. 18A:36-14 to 16) already lists it among religious holidays for which absences must be excused.

Do we need a lawyer?

No. This process starts with a respectful, well-written request from students to their own administration, and in our experience that is usually where it ends, with a yes. If a school pushes back, we help with the follow-up.

What if my principal says no?

A first "no" is not the end. We help reps figure out what the concern actually is, respond to it, and escalate thoughtfully if needed. Reach out through the form below and tell us where things stand.

Does my school have to close for Eid?

Closing is a school board decision, not a legal requirement, but your absence on Eid must be excused under NJ law. Around 30 NJ districts have already added Eid to their calendars as a day off, and pushing your board to join them is part of our long-term playbook.

Who is behind Prayer For All?

Students. We're a three-person founding team from New Jersey, and the project grew out of the NJMVP and Ali Leadership Institute Youth Leadership Program. The model is built so local MSA reps, not us, lead the effort at their own schools.

Does any of this cost money?

No. The playbook, templates, and our help are free for any student or MSA in New Jersey.
